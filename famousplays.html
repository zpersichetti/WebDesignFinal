Top 5 Famous Plays in NFL (this Decade)

February 3,2008 "The Helmet Catch"

In Super Bowl XLVII, the Giants had a third-and-five from their 44 with 1:15 left. New England led 14-10. Manning evaded defenders and threw the ball down the middle to Tyree, who went up and got two hands on the ball and wedged it against his helmet and retained control as he hit the ground at the New England 35.Four plays later, Manning connected with Plaxico Burress for a 13-yard touchdown to end the Patriots' bid for a perfect 19-0 season in the second greatest upset in Super Bowl history.

February 1, 2015 "Butler Interception"

The Seahawks trailed 28-24 in Super Bowl XLIX. On first down, Marshawn Lynch was stopped one yard short of the goal line. Russell Wilson fired to Ricardo Lockett, but Butler jumped the route two yards deep in the end zone, beat Lockett to the ball and won the Super Bowl.

February 5, 2017 "Circus Catch"

Tom Brady dropped back to pass with 2:28 to play in Super Bowl LI, trailing by eight points, 64 yards from the end zone. The first player to touch the quarterback’s pass was a Falcon, Robert Alford, the cornerback who earlier that night had snatched another of Brady’s passes and returned it for the second-longest pick-six in Super Bowl history. Alford got his hands on this one, too, but couldn’t pull it in. The ball spun around, end over end, through the air. As it tumbled toward the ground, at the 40-yard-line on the Atlanta side of the field, Edelman was outnumbered three to one but manages to catch the ball when the ball was inches from touching the floor.

November 23, 2014 "Catch of the Year"

During a game against the Dallas Cowboys on Sunday Night Football, Odell Beckham JR had 10 catches for 146 yards and two touchdowns. His one-handed touchdown reception was hailed as "catch of the year"

February 1, 2009 "The Longest Yard"

In Superbowl XLIII Pittsburgh Steelers linebacker James Harrison fakes a blitz and intercepts Kurt Warner's pass that was intended for Anquan Boldin at the goal line. He turns on the jets and runs 100 yards for the touchdown, which is the longest pick six in Superbowl History. Steelers eventually go on to win the game 27-23.
